01   Identity

Who Was Enoch?

Enoch is one of the most enigmatic figures in ancient literature. According to Genesis 5:24, he "walked with God, and was not, for God took him" — a mysterious departure that sparked centuries of speculation. He is the seventh generation from Adam, father of Methuselah, and grandfather of Noah.

7thGeneration from Adam
365Years — matching the solar year
2Humans who never died (with Elijah)
3,600+Languages the Bible is translated into

"And Enoch walked with God after he begat Methuselah three hundred years… and he was not; for God took him."

— Genesis 5:22–24

Unlike other patriarchs who simply died, Enoch was taken directly to heaven while still alive — placing him alongside Elijah as one of only two figures in the Hebrew Bible who never died. This translated status made him the ideal narrator for heavenly secrets, cosmic journeys, and divine mysteries. His name (חֲנוֹךְ, Ḥanōḵ) means "dedicated" or "initiated."

  • His lifespan of 365 years mirrors the solar year — likely intentional symbolism linking him to astronomical revelation
  • Mentioned in the New Testament (Hebrews 11:5, Jude 1:14–15) as a prophet
  • Venerated as a canonical prophet in Ethiopian Orthodox Christianity
  • The tradition of Enoch as heavenly scribe influenced later Jewish mysticism and early Christian thought
02   Structure

The Text & Its Five Books

1 Enoch (the Ethiopic Book of Enoch) is a composite work assembled over several centuries. It survived intact only in Ethiopic (Ge'ez), though Aramaic fragments were found among the Dead Sea Scrolls at Qumran — confirming its pre-Christian antiquity and its authority in the Qumran community.

📜
Chapters 1–36
The Book of the Watchers — fallen angels, the great flood's cause, and Enoch's guided tour of the cosmos and heavenly judgment halls.
⚖️
Chapters 37–71
The Book of Parables — the Son of Man enthroned in heaven, the fate of the wicked, and mysteries of the cosmos and its angelic rulers.
🌞
Chapters 72–82
The Astronomical Book — the oldest section. Uriel leads Enoch through the mechanics of the sun, moon, winds, and the 364-day calendar.
👁️
Chapters 83–90
The Book of Dream Visions — symbolic history from Adam to the Maccabean revolt, using animals as stand-ins for nations.
✉️
Chapters 91–108
The Epistle of Enoch — moral exhortation, the Apocalypse of Weeks dividing history into ten cosmic "weeks."
Key Insight

The Astronomical Book (chapters 72–82) is likely the oldest section, possibly pre-dating the rest by centuries. It reads less like prophecy and more like a technical manual — an ancient astronomer's notebook delivered through the voice of the angel Uriel.

03   Science

The Astronomical Book

Also called The Book of the Heavenly Luminaries, chapters 72–82 constitute the oldest surviving Jewish astronomical text. The angel Uriel ("Light of God") leads Enoch on a tour of the mechanisms governing the sun, moon, stars, and seasons.

"This is the first law of the luminaries: the luminary the Sun has its rising in the eastern portals of heaven and its setting in the western portals of heaven."

— 1 Enoch 72:2

The system described is a 364-day solar calendar divided into four seasons of 91 days each (13 weeks × 4). This calendar was also used by the Dead Sea Scroll community (the Essenes) and may reflect a priestly tradition that conflicted with the lunar calendar used in the Jerusalem Temple.

364Days in Enoch's solar calendar
6Gates for sunrise / 6 for sunset
4Seasons of 91 days each
11Dead Sea Scroll manuscripts found
  • The sun traverses 6 eastern "gates" and 6 western "gates" across the year
  • Days lengthen and shorten as the sun moves between gates — an early model of seasonal change
  • The moon is described with 14-day waxing and waning cycles
  • Stars are governed by angels who ensure they rise and set on time — tardiness is sin
  • Wind directions, rain, and dew are all assigned to specific portals and angelic custodians
04   Diagram

The Six Gates of the Sun

Enoch describes the eastern horizon as divided into six "portals" through which the sun rises at different times of year — an early geometric model of the sun's changing azimuth at sunrise. Modern analysis shows the angular values roughly correspond to the latitude of northern Mesopotamia or the Levant.

Eastern Horizon — Six Solar Gates

"In those days the sun rises from that portal and sets in the west, and returns to the east and rises in the third portal for one-and-thirty mornings."

— 1 Enoch 72:15
  • Gate I (far north): Summer solstice — longest day of the year
  • Gate III–IV: Spring and autumn equinoxes — equal day and night
  • Gate VI (far south): Winter solstice — shortest day
  • Each gate also governs specific weather: winds, snow, frost, dew, and rain
  • Daylight hours per month are tabulated, matching the solar declination at ~35°N latitude
05   The Fallen

The Watchers

The Watchers (Irin in Aramaic) are a class of angels assigned to observe and guard humanity. In 1 Enoch 6–16, two hundred of them descend to Mount Hermon, take human wives, and teach forbidden knowledge — setting off a chain of corruption that leads to the Great Flood.

"And they were in all two hundred; who descended in the days of Jared on the summit of Mount Hermon, and they called it Mount Hermon, because they had sworn and bound themselves by mutual imprecations upon it."

— 1 Enoch 6:6

Several Watchers are named specifically for celestial or meteorological phenomena:

⭐
Kokabiel
Angel of the Stars
His name derives from כּוֹכָב (kōḵāḇ), Hebrew for "star." Taught astrology and the movements of the constellations to humanity.
☀️
Shamsiel
Angel of the Sun
From שֶׁמֶשׁ (shemesh), "sun." One of the leaders of the fallen host; presided over the sun's course and taught solar lore.
⚡
Baraqiel
Angel of Lightning
From בָּרָק (baraq), "lightning." Taught the secrets of astrology alongside lightning and storm phenomena.
🌙
Saraqael
Angel over Spirits
Set over spirits who sin. Counted among the holy watchers in some traditions — the watcher who watched the watchers.
⚔️
Azâzêl
Chief of Forbidden Knowledge
Taught metalworking, weapons, and cosmetics — knowledge that turned humanity's attention from heaven to earth. Bound under the desert until the Day of Judgment.
06   Cosmology

The Seven Heavens

During his guided tour with Uriel and other angels, Enoch passes through multiple cosmic layers. While 1 Enoch describes a rich cosmology, later Enochic and related texts (2 Enoch, the Testament of Levi) elaborate the seven-heaven model that became foundational to Jewish mysticism and early Christian thought — including Paul's "third heaven" (2 Corinthians 12:2).

I First Heaven The firmament — weather angels governing wind, snow, rain, dew, and the clouds
II Second Heaven Realm of the fallen Watchers, awaiting judgment in darkness and silence
III Third Heaven Paradise on one side — a place of punishment on the other; the Garden, perfumed and golden
IV Fourth Heaven The sun's course, the moon's paths, and the twelve gates of the winds — the clockwork of the cosmos
V Fifth Heaven The Grigori — Watchers who did not descend, mourning in silence for their fallen brethren below
VI Sixth Heaven Angels governing nature: seasons, rivers, seas, crops, and the growth of all living things
VII Seventh Heaven The throne of God, the highest angels, and the heavenly books recording every human deed
07   Philosophy

Stars as Living Beings

One of the most striking features of Enochic cosmology is that stars are not inanimate objects — they are living beings, specifically angels, commanded to rise and set on schedule. Stars that deviate from their ordained paths are imprisoned and punished.

"And I saw there something like an invisible sea, and seven stars like great burning mountains, and when I inquired regarding them, the angel said: 'This place is the end of heaven and earth: this has become a prison for the stars and the host of heaven.'"

— 1 Enoch 18:14–15
Cosmic Ethics

In the Enochic worldview, astronomical regularity is not mechanical — it is moral. The stars obey because they choose to. Stars that rise "late" are condemned for transgression, using the same language as the fallen Watchers. Cosmic order is a form of worship; chaos is always the result of disobedience.

  • Each star has a name, a corresponding angel, and an assigned orbit
  • Stars that rise "late" are condemned for transgression — moral language applied to physics
  • Uriel is specifically the angel set over all the luminaries of heaven
  • The regularity of celestial motion is proof of divine order — not clockwork, but obedience
08   Influence

Legacy & Influence

The Book of Enoch was widely read across the ancient world. Fragments from eleven separate manuscripts were found among the Dead Sea Scrolls — more copies than most books of the Hebrew Bible — indicating it was treated as authoritative scripture by the Qumran community.

📖
New Testament
Jude 1:14–15 quotes 1 Enoch 1:9 directly, calling Enoch "the seventh from Adam" and a prophet. The New Testament's angelology is deeply shaped by Enochic tradition.
📜
Dead Sea Scrolls
11 manuscripts discovered at Qumran (1947–1956), confirming the Aramaic original pre-dates the Common Era and was authoritative for the Essene community.
⛪
Ethiopian Church
1 Enoch remains canonical scripture in the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church — the only major Christian tradition to retain it in its full Bible.
🔭
History of Science
Modern scholars regard the Astronomical Book as evidence that ancient Jewish communities engaged seriously with observational astronomy, encoding technical knowledge in religious narrative.
🕍
Jewish Mysticism
The seven-heaven model and the Merkabah (chariot) mysticism of rabbinic Judaism trace directly to Enochic traditions — the divine throne-room vision is a direct descendant.

"Blessed is the man who dies in righteousness and goodness, concerning whom there is no book of unrighteousness written, and against whom no day of judgement shall be found."

— 1 Enoch 81:4
