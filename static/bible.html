01   Overview

What Is the Bible?

The Bible is a collection of 66 books written across roughly 1,500 years in Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek. It is the best-selling and most translated book in human history, and the foundational text of Judaism and Christianity.

~1,500Years of composition

The Old Testament (Hebrew Bible / Tanakh) contains the Torah (Law), Nevi'im (Prophets), and Ketuvim (Writings). The New Testament records the life of Jesus, the early church, letters to communities, and apocalyptic prophecy in Revelation.

  • The Torah is shared as authoritative scripture by Judaism, Christianity, and Islam
  • The oldest complete manuscript of the Hebrew Bible is the Leningrad Codex (1008–1009 CE)
  • Dead Sea Scrolls (discovered 1947) contain fragments going back to the 3rd century BCE
  • The Septuagint (LXX), a Greek translation begun c. 280 BCE in Alexandria, became the primary Bible of early Christianity

"God made two great lights — the greater light to govern the day and the lesser light to govern the night. He also made the stars."

— Genesis 1:16
Notable Detail

The sun and moon are conspicuously unnamed in Genesis 1 — called only "the greater light" and "the lesser light." Ancient Near Eastern cultures deified these bodies under names like Shamash (sun) and Sin (moon). The deliberate avoidance of their names may be a theological statement: they are lamps God created, not gods.

03   Cosmology

The Firmament & Ancient Cosmos

The biblical cosmos is a three-tiered structure. The Hebrew word raqia — translated "firmament" or "expanse" — is a solid dome separating the waters above from the earth below. Rain falls when God opens the "windows of heaven."

Ancient Biblical Cosmology — Cross Section
🌌 Upper Waters Waters above the firmament — source of rain; the divine realm, throne of God
🔵 The Raqia (Firmament) Solid dome; sun, moon, and stars set within it like lights; "windows" opened for rain
🌬️ Lower Atmosphere Wind, clouds, weather, birds — the breathable air between firmament and ground
🏔️ Earth Flat disk resting on pillars, surrounded by the great ocean; the seas, land, and mountains
🕳️ Sheol / The Deep The underworld realm of the dead; the subterranean ocean from which springs arise

"He set the earth on its foundations; it can never be moved… He established the heavens, when he drew a circle on the face of the deep."

— Psalm 104:5 / Proverbs 8:27
  • The "windows of heaven" open in Noah's flood (Genesis 7:11) — rain is stored water released from above
  • Earth rests on pillars set in the deep (1 Samuel 2:8, Job 9:6)
  • This cosmological model was shared across ancient Mesopotamia, Egypt, and Canaan
  • Proverbs 8:27 describes God drawing "a circle on the face of the deep" — some scholars read this as a spherical earth, others as a cosmic horizon
04   Star Lore

Stars & Constellations

The Bible contains some of the earliest named references to constellations in any literature. The book of Job — one of the oldest texts in the Bible — names several star groups and challenges the reader with their mysteries.

"Can you bind the chains of the Pleiades? Can you loosen Orion's belt? Can you bring forth the constellations in their seasons or lead out the Bear with its cubs?"

— Job 38:31–32
Pleiades (Kimah)

The "bound" cluster — rainy season marker in Canaan

Orion (Kesil)

Meaning "fool" or "giant" — associated with winter storms

✨
Pleiades (Kimah)
The "bound" cluster. God challenges Job: can you bind its chains? The Pleiades' rising marked the rainy season in Canaan.
⚔️
Orion (Kesil)
Meaning "fool" or "giant" — loosening his belt is beyond human power. Associated with winter storms and brute strength.
🐻
The Bear (Ash)
Likely Ursa Major — led across the northern sky with its cubs. The circumpolar stars that never set, always circling the pole.
🌌
Mazzaroth
Job 38:32 — possibly the 12 zodiac signs or the full circuit of constellations. Their seasonal appearances are God's alone to orchestrate.
05   Events

Astronomical Events in Scripture

Several passages describe dramatic interventions in the normal behaviour of celestial bodies — events that have fascinated astronomers, historians, and theologians for centuries.

Joshua's Long Day
Joshua 10:12–14
The sun "stood still" over Gibeon and the moon over the Valley of Aijalon for "about a full day." Proposed explanations include a solar eclipse, atmospheric refraction, poetic metaphor, or actual astronomical anomaly. Ancient Chinese and Egyptian records have been searched for corroborating events.
Hezekiah's Shadow
2 Kings 20:9–11 / Isaiah 38:8
As a sign to the dying King Hezekiah, the shadow on the sundial of Ahaz moved backward ten steps. Josephus and early interpreters debated whether this was a local miracle or a global astronomical reversal.
The Star of Bethlehem
Matthew 2:1–12
Magi from the east follow a star to Jesus' birthplace. Candidates include the Jupiter–Saturn conjunction of 7 BCE, a comet recorded by Chinese astronomers around 5 BCE, a nova, or Jupiter's retrograde motion through Virgo in 3–2 BCE. The debate remains open.
Darkness at the Crucifixion
Matthew 27:45 / Luke 23:44
Three hours of darkness over the land from noon to 3 PM. A solar eclipse is ruled out by chronology — Passover falls on a full moon, when a solar eclipse is impossible. Historians note a possible Mediterranean dust event; others treat it as a miraculous sign.
Blood Moon & Solar Darkening
Joel 2:31 / Acts 2:20 / Revelation 6:12
Recurring prophetic imagery of the sun turning dark and the moon turning to blood — likely describing lunar eclipses (which give the moon a reddish hue) combined with volcanic dust or solar eclipse events as signs of the Day of the Lord.
06   Celestial Beings

The Heavenly Host

The term heavenly host (צְבָא הַשָּׁמַיִם) refers both to angels and to the stars themselves — the two were often synonymous. Worshipping the "host of heaven" is the most frequently condemned form of idolatry in the Old Testament.

🔥
Seraphim
Six-winged beings of the divine throne
Surrounding God's throne in Isaiah 6, calling "Holy, holy, holy." Their name means "burning ones" — possibly connected to lightning or the stars.
👁️
Cherubim
Four-faced, four-winged guardians
Guarding Eden, the Ark of the Covenant, and the divine throne. Ezekiel's vision makes them spectacularly cosmic: wheels within wheels covered in eyes, moving in all directions.
⚔️
Michael
Archangel, warrior, guardian of Israel
"The great prince who protects your people" (Daniel 12:1). Leads the heavenly army against the dragon in Revelation 12:7. His name means "Who is like God?"
📣
Gabriel
Messenger, divine herald
Brings news to Daniel, Zechariah (John's birth), and Mary (Jesus' birth). His name means "God is my strength." He stands "in the presence of God" — one of only four named angels in canonical scripture.
👑
Bene Elohim (Sons of God)
Divine council members
Present themselves before God in Job 1–2; "shouted for joy" at creation (Job 38:7). In Genesis 6:1–4, some take human wives — the tradition expanded in 1 Enoch as the Watchers.

"Praise him, all his angels; praise him, all his heavenly hosts. Praise him, sun and moon; praise him, all you shining stars."

— Psalm 148:2–3
07   Visions

Prophetic & Apocalyptic Visions

The prophetic books contain some of the most cosmically vivid writing in ancient literature. Ezekiel, Daniel, and Revelation describe heavenly realms and the ultimate fate of the cosmos in imagery that has captivated readers for millennia.

"I looked, and I saw a windstorm coming out of the north — an immense cloud with flashing lightning and surrounded by brilliant light. The center of the fire looked like glowing metal, and in the fire was what looked like four living creatures."

— Ezekiel 1:4–5
🔥
Ezekiel's Chariot
Four living creatures bearing a crystal expanse, above which sits a sapphire throne. Wheels within wheels, covered in eyes. The Merkabah — the foundational vision of Jewish mysticism.
👴
Daniel's Ancient of Days
God on a fiery throne, surrounded by the heavenly court. The "Son of Man" approaches on clouds to receive eternal dominion (Daniel 7:9–14) — one of the most influential passages in all apocalyptic literature.
🐉
Revelation's Cosmic War
The dragon sweeps a third of the stars from heaven. The sun, moon, and stars are darkened or fall. Seven-sealed scrolls open to cosmic catastrophe. A woman clothed with the sun, standing on the moon.
🌟
New Heavens & New Earth
Isaiah 65:17 and Revelation 21 promise a complete cosmic renewal: the first heaven and earth pass away, replaced by a new creation — the ultimate end of the biblical cosmological narrative.

"Then I saw a new heaven and a new earth, for the first heaven and the first earth had passed away, and there was no longer any sea."

— Revelation 21:1
08   Influence

Legacy & Influence

The Bible's influence on science, astronomy, law, art, literature, music, and political philosophy is without parallel. For over a millennium, the dominant model of the cosmos in Western civilisation was one in which a personal God created, ordered, and sustained the universe.

#1Best-selling book in history
~800CE: First European universities founded on biblical learning
5B+Copies printed
  • The concept of "laws of nature" — essential to scientific thinking — has roots in the biblical idea of a rational Creator who orders the cosmos consistently
  • Copernicus, Galileo, Kepler, Newton, and Faraday all operated within a theistic framework shaped by biblical cosmology
  • The seven-day week, now universal across virtually all human cultures, derives directly from the Genesis creation account
  • Homer, Dante, Milton, Shakespeare, Dostoevsky, Toni Morrison — the Western literary canon is incomprehensible without biblical literacy
  • More hospitals, schools, and universities have been founded in the name of biblical teaching than any other single intellectual tradition
